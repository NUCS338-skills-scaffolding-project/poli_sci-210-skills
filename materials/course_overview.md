Course Overview
This course introduces the empirical methods used by political scientists to answer research questions,
including their appropriate application, advantages, and shortcomings. Students learn how researchers
use data in social science contexts, with a particular emphasis on political science topics, across three
broad areas: quantitative, experimental, and qualitative studies. The course prepares students to
read and evaluate research in subsequent courses on substantive social science topics.

Learning Objectives
By the end of the course, students should be able to:
•

Identify and explain descriptive and causal inference approaches, as well as their challenges in
political science.

•

Evaluate inferential claims in academic social science research.

•

Identify types of research designs, explain their strengths and weaknesses, and discuss alternative
approaches.

•

Communicate research processes and findings.

Requirements & Distribution
There are no prerequisites. The course fulfills the methodology requirement for Political Science majors
and counts toward the Empirical and Deductive Reasoning requirement (formerly the Formal Studies
Distribution Area) at Weinberg College.

Research Study Participation Requirement
Separately from the graded assignments, students must complete a research participation assignment
of up to 4 hours, consisting of studies run by Political Science students and faculty (mostly online
surveys, occasionally in-person at Scott Hall). Students who prefer not to participate may opt for an
alternative: read a book chapter (~20 pages) and write a 5-page reaction paper. To opt in to the paper
alternative, email lab manager Jacob Fortier (jacobfortier2027@u.northwestern.edu).
