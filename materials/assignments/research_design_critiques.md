Purpose
Every week has additional non-textbook readings — usually published articles that illustrate the
application of the methods covered that week. The critique asks students to engage closely with one of
these studies and apply course material to evaluate how it was designed and executed.

What the Syllabus Requires
A short-form document (~700–1,000 words) that:
•

Summarizes the study's research question.

•

Explains the study's relevance.

•

Describes the study's research design.

•

Uses course material to evaluate the design or implementation.

•

Ultimately answers: “What should we change if we were to go back and conduct the study
ourselves?”

How Critiques Fit the Course Learning Objectives
This assignment directly targets three of the four course objectives: evaluating inferential claims in
academic social science research; identifying research designs and explaining their strengths,
weaknesses, and alternatives; and communicating research processes and findings in writing.
